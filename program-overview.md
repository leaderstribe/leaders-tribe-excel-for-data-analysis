# Program Overview: Excel for Data Analysis for Women

---

## Background

In a world that is becoming increasingly data-driven, access to data skills is no longer a nice-to-have — it is essential. Yet women continue to face significant gaps in access to technology training and digital opportunities.

**Leaders Tribe** created the *Excel for Data Analysis for Women* program to address this gap head-on. By combining practical Excel skills training with a leadership development lens, this program helps women not only learn to work with data, but to lead with it.

---

## Program Goal

To empower women with beginner to intermediate skills in data analysis using Microsoft Excel, while building leadership confidence and digital literacy to support full participation in the global digital economy.

---

## Specific Objectives

By the end of the program, participants will be able to:

- Apply Microsoft Excel skills proficiently for data entry, processing, analysis, and visualization
- Use intermediate Excel functions including lookup functions, conditional functions, and PivotTables
- Draw conclusions and communicate results based on data interpretation
- Demonstrate digital leadership by mentoring other learners through the Train-the-Trainer model

---

## Target Participants

**Primary Audience:** Women aged 18 and above, from any country, with access to a laptop and internet connection.

**Backgrounds:** Students, early-career professionals, entrepreneurs, NGO staff, and community advocates seeking to improve their digital and analytical skills.

**Scale:** 20 participants per cohort × 2 cohorts annually (40 women per year).

---

## Program Structure

The program is delivered across two progressive levels, running over eight weeks per cohort (two sessions per week, 1.5 hours each).

---

### Level 1 — Beginner: Building Confidence with Excel

**Duration:** 4 weeks | 2 sessions per week | 1.5 hours per session

**Theme:** From Zero to Familiar

**Focus areas:**
- Excel navigation and interface
- Data entry, sorting, filtering, and basic cleaning
- Core formulas: SUM, AVERAGE, MIN, MAX
- IF statements and logical thinking in Excel
- Charts and basic data visualization
- Conditional formatting and data summarization

**Capstone Project:** *Everyday Data Story* — a one-page Excel report using a real or provided dataset, including a cleaned table, at least three formulas, one chart, and a short written data story.

---

### Train-the-Trainer (TTT) — The Bridge Between Levels

The Train-the-Trainer model is a core feature of the program design. It transforms every Level 1 graduate into a community knowledge multiplier.

**Requirements:**
- Redeliver the Level 1 training to at least **10 new learners** in your community or professional network
- Ensure at least **50% of those learners are women**
- Submit a **TTT Reflection Report** documenting sessions held, learner count, photos, and key takeaways

**Why it matters:** Every woman who completes Level 1 and fulfils the TTT requirement helps grow a ripple effect of digital empowerment — reaching far beyond the original cohort.

**Certificate:** The Level 1 Certificate and access to Level 2 are granted upon successful completion of the capstone and TTT requirement.

---

### Level 2 — Intermediate: From Data to Insight

**Duration:** 4 weeks | 2 sessions per week | 1.5 hours per session

**Theme:** Clean Data, Clear Insights → Insight to Action

**Focus areas:**
- Advanced data cleaning: Remove Duplicates, Text-to-Columns, Flash Fill, Find & Replace
- Data validation and dropdown lists
- Lookup functions: VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, INDEX-MATCH
- Conditional functions: COUNTIF, SUMIF, AVERAGEIF, IFS, IFERROR
- PivotTables and PivotCharts
- Interactive dashboards with slicers and timelines

**Capstone Project:** *Community Data in Action* — a fully interactive Excel dashboard built from a real-world or provided dataset, including data cleaning, lookup formulas, a PivotTable, and a short written data interpretation.

---

## Expected Outcomes

**Short-Term**
- 40 women trained annually in beginner and intermediate Excel data analysis
- Improved confidence using digital tools for analysis and decision-making

**Medium-Term**
- Participants apply Excel skills to their work, education, or small business operations
- Women-led peer learning groups established to mentor new learners

**Long-Term**
- A growing global network of women using data to inform projects, lead initiatives, and create community impact
- Graduates recognized as **Certified Women Data Ambassadors** under Leaders Tribe

---

## Sustainability Model

The program is built to be self-sustaining through three pillars:

**Financial:** A hybrid model combining grants and sponsorships in early cohorts, with tiered participation fees and scholarships as the program scales.

**Institutional:** The TTT model ensures exponential reach — every graduate becomes a facilitator, building a growing pipeline of trained women who can co-facilitate future cohorts.

**Community:** Alumni form peer learning circles, access a global opportunities network, and are recognized through a formal certification pathway.

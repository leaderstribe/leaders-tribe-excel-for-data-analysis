# Train-the-Trainer (TTT) Guide

---

## What Is the Train-the-Trainer Requirement?

The **Train-the-Trainer (TTT) model** is a core part of the *Excel for Data Analysis for Women* program. It is not optional — it is the bridge between Level 1 and Level 2.

The TTT requirement is built on a simple but powerful idea: **every woman who learns becomes a woman who teaches.** By requiring participants to redeliver the Level 1 training before advancing, the program creates an ever-growing network of data-literate women who can mentor and support others in their communities.

---

## Who Must Complete the TTT Requirement?

All participants who complete the Level 1 capstone project and wish to:

- Receive the **Level 1 Excel Beginner Certificate**, and/or
- Advance to **Level 2: Intermediate**

must fulfill the TTT requirement in full.

---

## TTT Requirements at a Glance

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Minimum learners | At least **10 new learners** |
| Gender inclusion | At least **50% of learners must be women** |
| Delivery setting | Community group, workplace, school, or peer network |
| Reporting | Submit a completed **TTT Reflection Report** |
| Deadline | Before the start of Level 2 (or by the cohort deadline set by your facilitator) |

---

## What You Will Teach

As a TTT facilitator, you will redeliver the **Level 1 Beginner content**, covering:

- Excel navigation and basic formatting
- Data entry, sorting, filtering, and basic cleaning
- Core formulas: SUM, AVERAGE, MIN, MAX
- IF statements
- Charts and simple data visualization
- A hands-on activity or group exercise

You do not need to deliver all eight sessions. A condensed 2–4 session version covering the core skills is acceptable, provided all major topics are included.

---

## How to Plan Your TTT Sessions

### Step 1: Identify Your Learners
Think about who in your community, school, or workplace could benefit. This could be colleagues, friends, fellow students, church or mosque groups, market women, NGO teammates, or anyone interested in learning Excel.

### Step 2: Choose a Setting
Your sessions can be:
- In-person (at home, a library, community center, or workplace)
- Online (via Zoom, Google Meet, or WhatsApp video)
- A mix of both

### Step 3: Plan Your Sessions
Use the session outlines in `facilitator-resources/` and the Level 1 materials in `level-1/` to plan your delivery. Simplify where needed for your audience.

### Step 4: Gather Evidence
Keep a record of:
- Names of learners who attended (or a count if privacy is a concern)
- Dates and duration of each session
- At least one or two photos from a session
- A short reflection on how it went and what you learned from teaching

### Step 5: Submit Your TTT Reflection Report
Use the **TTT Reflection Report Template** in `train-the-trainer/ttt-reflection-report-template.md` and submit it to your Leaders Tribe facilitator.

---

## TTT Reflection Report — What to Include

Your reflection report should cover:

1. **Your name and cohort**
2. **Total number of learners reached**
3. **Number of women among your learners**
4. **Sessions delivered** — dates, duration, and topics covered
5. **Setting and format** — where and how you delivered (online, in-person, etc.)
6. **One or two photos** (optional but encouraged)
7. **Key takeaways** — What went well? What was challenging? What did you learn from teaching?
8. **Impact statement** — In 2–3 sentences, describe the difference this experience made for your learners and for you

---

## What Happens After You Submit

Once your facilitator reviews and approves your TTT Reflection Report:

- You will receive your **Level 1 Excel Beginner Certificate**
- You will be enrolled in **Level 2: Intermediate**
- Your contribution will be logged in the Leaders Tribe community impact record

Outstanding TTT facilitators may be invited to join the Leaders Tribe team as **volunteer co-facilitators** for future cohorts.

---

## TTT Tips for Success

- **Keep it practical.** Your learners don't need a lecture — they need to open Excel and try things.
- **Use relatable examples.** Choose data that feels familiar to your group — market sales, attendance sheets, household budgets.
- **Move at their pace.** Patience is more powerful than speed.
- **Celebrate small wins.** Completing their first formula is a big deal. Treat it like one.
- **Ask for help.** If you get stuck on a topic, reach out to your Leaders Tribe facilitator — that's what they're there for.

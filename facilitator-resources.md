## Running Sessions Effectively

### General Facilitation Principles

- **Start with context.** Open each session by connecting the skill to real life. Ask: *Where would someone use this in their work or business?*
- **Model first, then practice.** Demonstrate the skill on-screen before asking participants to try it themselves.
- **Use the provided datasets.** The practice datasets are designed to match each session's skill — don't swap them out without checking they still work for the exercise.
- **Build in reflection.** At the end of each session, ask: *What was one thing you learned today? What would you still like to understand better?*
- **Create psychological safety.** Remind participants regularly that there are no silly questions and that errors are part of learning.

### Pacing Guidance

| Session Type | Suggested Pacing |
|---|---|
| Introduction sessions (Week 1) | Move slower — many participants may be brand new to Excel |
| Formula sessions (Week 2) | Allow extra time for practice and common error troubleshooting |
| Chart and visualization sessions (Week 3) | Move at medium pace; focus on interpretation, not just creation |
| Capstone sessions (Week 4) | Use as open working time; circulate and support individually |

## Managing Common Challenges

### Participants with different skill levels
- Pair faster learners with those who need support during group activities
- Provide optional extension tasks for participants who finish early (e.g., "Can you do this with a different formula?")

### Technical issues (internet, software)
- Ask participants to have Excel open before the session starts
- Prepare a screenshot or static version of key steps in case screen-sharing breaks
- Keep a WhatsApp group active so participants can ask questions asynchronously

### Participants falling behind
- The mini-assignments are not graded — they're for practice. Encourage rather than penalize late submissions.
- Share a brief "catch-up" summary at the start of the next session covering the previous week's key concepts.

## Assessment Guidance

### Pre-Assessment
Administer at the start of Level 1 (and again at the start of Level 2). It captures baseline knowledge and helps facilitators gauge the group's starting point. It is not graded and should feel low-stakes.

### Mini-Assignments
One per session. Purpose:
- Give participants structured practice
- Allow facilitators to identify who may need extra support
- Build toward the capstone

Feedback should be encouraging and specific. Focus on what the participant got right, then offer one concrete improvement suggestion.

### Capstone Assessment
Use the rubric in `level-1/assessments/` or `level-2/assessments/` to evaluate:

**Level 1 Capstone Rubric areas:**
- Data is cleaned and formatted (20%)
- At least three formulas or functions used correctly (30%)
- One meaningful chart created (25%)
- Written data story (3–5 sentences) is clear and accurate (25%)

**Level 2 Capstone Rubric areas:**
- Data cleaning applied thoroughly (20%)
- Lookup and conditional functions used correctly (25%)
- PivotTable and PivotChart included (25%)
- Dashboard is functional and readable (20%)
- Written insight summary (5 sentences) is clear and data-driven (10%)

## Monitoring and Reporting

Facilitators should maintain:
- **Attendance logs** for every session (template in `templates/`)
- **Assessment records** (pre and post scores)
- **TTT report submissions** from participants
- **Capstone evaluation scores**

These records feed into the Leaders Tribe monitoring and evaluation system. See [Monitoring and Evaluation](monitoring-and-evaluation.md) for more.

## Facilitator Self-Care

Facilitation is rewarding but can be tiring. A few reminders:

- You do not need to know the answer to every question in the moment. "Let me check that and come back to you" is always a valid response.
- Celebrate your participants' growth — it is a direct reflection of your effort.
- Connect with other facilitators in the Leaders Tribe network to share ideas and support each other.
